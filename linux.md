# Before the Linux Install Event

Make sure you've done the following before you come along:

## Back Up Your Files
Copy everything you want to keep — documents, photos, music, downloads — to an external hard drive or USB stick. **Installing Linux will wipe your drive, so this is essential.** The simplest way is to plug in your drive and drag and drop your important folders across in File Explorer. Do it twice if you're unsure.

As an extra safety net, check whether you already have files synced to Google Drive, OneDrive or iCloud — many people do without realising it.

## Save Your Browser Bookmarks
Export your bookmarks before you arrive so you don't lose them:
- **Chrome / Edge:** Menu → Bookmarks → Export Bookmarks
- **Firefox:** Menu → Bookmarks → Manage Bookmarks → Import and Backup → Export

## Back Up Your Passwords
If you use a password manager like Bitwarden, 1Password, or LastPass, your passwords are already safe and you don't need to do anything.

Otherwise, export your saved passwords from your browser:
- **Chrome / Edge:** Settings → Password Manager → click the menu icon → Export
- **Firefox:** Type `about:logins` in the address bar → menu in the top right → Export Logins
- **Safari:** File → Export → Passwords

> ⚠️ The exported file contains all your passwords in plain text — keep it on your backup drive and treat it carefully.

## Note Down Your Wi-Fi Details
You'll need your home Wi-Fi network name and password to get back online after the install. It's usually on a sticker on your router.

## Think About the Software You Use
Make a note of any programs you rely on day-to-day — things like Microsoft Office, Zoom, Spotify, or anything work-related. Bring the list along and we'll help you get set up, or if necessary, we'll help find Linux alternatives on the day.

---

## On the Day, Bring:
- Your laptop
- Your charger / power adapter
- Your backup drive (if you have one!)
- Your notes (Wi-Fi details, software list, passwords)
